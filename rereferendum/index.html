Mapping Discontent


On the 23rd of June 2016 Britain voted to leave the European Union.

The final results were:

Leave: 17,410,742
Remain: 16,141,241

Dissatified with the result, millions of people have signed a petition calling for a second referendum.

This map combines contituency boundary data from the Office for National Statistics with live petition data from the Houses of Parliament to provide a geographic view of signatories. Not unlike the referendum itself, the regional differences are clear.

The figures shown for 'Total Signatures' and 'Non-UK Signatures' are live and will update dynamically as people sign the petition. Of course, the rate of new additions is likely to decrease over time and the petition will eventually be closed.

Interesting future work could compare these figures to the actual referendum results. In particular, normalising these figures to either the raw 'Leave' or 'Remain' votes may highlight any anomalous areas. Incorporating the petition signatures from overseas could also add an interesting perspective.
